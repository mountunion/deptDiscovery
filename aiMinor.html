Department of Mathematics and Computer Science

Artificial Intelligence Minor:

Courses Required for the minor in Artificial Intelligence:

 		  About the AI minor:
    One of the following 2-course sequences:  either --
        CSC 120   Programming and Problem Solving I
        CSC 220   Programming and Problem Solving II
      -- or --
        DSC 140   Data Science Fundamentals
        DSC 250   Scientific Modeling and Data Analysis
    DSC 340   Machine Learning and Neural Network Processing
    CSC 480   Artificial Intelligence
    and one of the following courses:
        CSC 320   Algorithms and Data Structures
        CSC 450   Theory of Computation
        MTH 125   Elementary Discrete Mathematics
        MTH 223   Intermediate Statistics, or
        MTH 322   Linear Algebra

Total Credit Hours required:  20

Note:  only 8 credits from the courses above may be counted toward another major or minor program.

   The Artificial Intelligence minor will enable you to develop technical expertise in one of this generation's most in-demand fields.  As our world becomes more familiar with this innovative landscape, the need for professionals with AI expertise is growing rapidly.  This minor program will prepare you to build customized AI solutions and pursue career paths that require AI specialization.  Hands-on projects will challenge you to develop skills in areas like language comprehension, computer vision, machine learning and finding hidden patterns in complex data.
